What we cover

Every kind of solar install.

Whatever your roof and energy goals, our matched installers can scope, size, and quote.

Solar PV (panels)

Photovoltaic panels generate electricity from sunlight. Typical 3-bed UK home: 8–12 panels (3–5kW system). Saves £400–£1,200/yr on electricity bills.

Solar PV + battery

Pairs panels with battery storage so you use solar power after dark. 5kWh battery typical. Bigger savings, more energy independence.

Solar thermal

Roof-mounted collectors heat water (not electricity). Less common now but useful as part of a hybrid heat-pump or boiler system.

FAQ

Solar questions, answered.

How much does solar cost in the UK?
Standard 4kW solar PV install runs £6,000–£8,500. Add a 5kWh battery and you're looking at £10,000–£14,000 total. With 0% VAT (until 2027) you save another ~£1,200–£2,800 vs pre-2022 pricing. Tradesly is free for homeowners — installers pay us a small matching fee per quote, never you.
What's the payback period?
Most UK solar installs pay back in 8–12 years through energy savings + Smart Export Guarantee (selling surplus back to the grid). Adding a battery shortens daytime export but lengthens overall payback by 1–2 years — in exchange for higher self-consumption and resilience.
What is MCS certification and why does it matter?
MCS (Microgeneration Certification Scheme) certifies installers and equipment. It's required to qualify for the Smart Export Guarantee and any government incentives. Every solar installer on Tradesly is MCS-certified — we don't list anyone who isn't.
Are Tradesly installers vetted?
Every solar installer on Tradesly is MCS-certified, insured, and held to a quality bar. Installers who let customers down are paused automatically based on dispute reports. We don't list directories or unverified businesses.
