FAQ

Scaffolding questions, answered.

How much does scaffolding cost in the UK?
Domestic 3-storey rear scaffold (typical re-roof job): £600–£950 first 2 weeks, then ~£20–£40/week extension. Whole-house scaffold: £1,200–£2,800 for 2 weeks. Quotes include erect, dismantle, and £5m public liability insurance. Tradesly is free for homeowners — scaffolders pay us a small matching fee per quote.
What's included in the hire period?
Standard quotes cover 2 weeks. Extensions are weekly thereafter (~£20–£40/week). If your roofer slips dates, your scaffolder will hold for the extra time at the weekly rate. Scaffolds are tagged and weekly-inspected during long hires.
Are scaffolders properly trained and insured?
Every scaffold firm on Tradesly is CISRS-trained (Construction Industry Scaffolders Record Scheme — the UK industry standard), £5m+ public liability insured, and held to a quality bar. We don't list anyone without these credentials.
How fast can it be erected?
Most domestic scaffolds erected within 24–48 hours of acceptance, weather dependent. Same-week is standard in most UK postcodes. Tell us the date you need scaffold up and we'll match firms with availability.
